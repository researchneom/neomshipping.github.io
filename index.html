Regional in Focus

Regional Market Overview

Freight conditions remained highly route-specific during the week, with renewed regional uncertainty influencing cargo enquiries, vessel positioning and chartering decisions.

Regional activity remained mixed this week, with market dynamics varying across individual trade lanes rather than showing a broad-based recovery. Cargo enquiries from the AG, which had gradually started to resurface following the ceasefire, slowed considerably following renewed regional tensions.

Market participation became more measured as both owners and charterers reassessed freight economics and operational considerations before committing to fresh business, with the number of vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz reportedly falling back towards levels seen before the ceasefire.

Only a limited number of fixtures were concluded from the AG during the week. A reported 20 KT caustic soda cargo from Jubail to Ras Al Khair was fixed at around USD 1.25 million lumpsum, underscoring the premium owners continued to command for Gulf export employment.

The limited inflow of inward ballasters gradually reduced the pool of prompt vessels within the AG, making it increasingly difficult for charterers to secure suitable tonnage for prompt requirements. Enquiries from the WCI into the AG also began to emerge, although several participants remained cautious while awaiting successful Strait of Hormuz transits.
